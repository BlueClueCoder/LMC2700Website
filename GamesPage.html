How to Explain a Game

If you're "the game guy", the person responsible for bringing and teaching people the rules to board games, you've no doubt dealt with the struggle of quickly and effectively explaining a game. You have to walk the line between making sure everyone has a fair understanding of how to play, and not boring them with a 30 minute lecture. The channel Shut Up & Sit Down has an excellent video on this topic, which I've linked here.

But if you don't have time for that, here is a basic summary.

  • Know how to play before teaching.
  • Allow your friends to touch the game's pieces.
  • Say WHO we are, HOW we win, and WHY this game is fun.
  • Use people's names when you give examples.
  • Rehearse your explanation before people show up.
  • Encourage and reward questions. EXAMPLE: "That's a really good question!"
  • If someone else knows how to play, collaborate instead of talking over one another.
  • Play a "fake" round to clear up misconceptions.
  • Begin game night with a shorter, simpler game.

Future Prospects

Here at Georgia Tech, I am majoring in Computational Media, and am focusing on the threads of Games and Media. My dream career is to take part in the creation of board and video games. Right now, I am enrolled in a Games Studies course, and have had the experience of designing a prototype.

This gave me insight into the process of brainstorming, designing, developing, playtesting, and revising a game. What was most encouraging to me was that I loved every second of it. This project dismissed any doubts I had about pursuing the wrong major.
