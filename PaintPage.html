Painting

I've enjoyed trying to paint since highschool. I'm still an amateur, but it's a nice way to relax while still creating something new.

My Work

Most of the paintings done on canvas are heavily based off of videos I watched on YouTube. This hobby also crosses over into my love of board games. I quickly discovered an entire body of knowledge focused around painting the miniatures that come with the games.

What I've Learned

Below is a list of the general concepts I've picked up. I really push myself to paint thoughtfully, that is, to actually think about the techniques I'm using while I paint a canvas or miniature. I still get lazy, but I think I'm getting better because I see the results.

  • Start painting every model by spraying it with black or white primer.
  • Paint your model one color at a time, and don't go back for mistakes until you've cycled through each color once.
  • Don't worry if your base coat looks flat. Washes and hilights will fix this.
  • After adding a base coat, washes can be your best friend in giving your mini shadow and depth.
  • Use hilighting at the very end to pick out one or two important pieces of your mini.
  • Don't over apply your spray finish, or your model will have a "wet" look.

Ways to Learn More

These are some of my favorite channels that help with either painting knowledge or inspiration.

  • Miniac: great advice on painting miniatures.
  • Wow Art: one of the first canvas painting channels I found. Easy to follow.
  • Joni Young Art: does remarkable fantasy paintings, more time-intensive.
  • Goobertown Hobbies: walks you through a comprehensive but easy to follow mini painting tutorial.
